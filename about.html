Vision

We all know that we serve a God of plan and purpose. And His plan for us is settled in heaven forever. Therefore, locating God’s plan for one’s life is the foundation for true fulfillment.
Back in 1977, I came across a verse of scripture that turned my life around dramatically. God said,
"for I know the plan that I have for you says the Lord, a plan of welfare and not of evil to give you a future and a hope." Jer.29:11(RSV)
Speaking to me from this scripture, God said, "My son, your future is in my plan not in your plan and until you discover my plan and pursue it, you don’t have a future." Remember , Godsaid,
"I am the Lord I change not"(Mal3:6)
-He had a plan for Jeremiah before he was born – Jer.1:5
-He separated Paul to the gospel while he was yet in his mother’s womb – Gal1:15-16

Surely, God has a plan for all his children. And as we all know that you don’t determine the purpose of a product after it has arrived in the market; it must have been decided right from the factory. In the same vein, God’s plan and purpose for everyone of us has been fully packaged before we arrived here on earth and all we need is to discover and pursue the same.
We further understand from scriptures that everychild of God is a child of destiny. As it is written;
"For whom he did foreknow, heal so did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son, that he might be the first born among many brethren. Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified."Rom8:29—30 (Seealso,Rom9:10-24)
From the above scripture, every child of God is predestinated to be glorified. We have been called unto glory and honour (2Pet1:3).
We have an enviable destiny (Gen26:14, Gal4:28).
We are to be envied and not to be pitied. (1Pet2:9)
Therefore accessing God’s plan is the gateway to a glorious destiny. We must recognize that we serve a God of preordination (Jer.1:5); a God of predestination (Rom.9:10-14); a God who knows the end from the beginning (Is.46:9-10)
We must also recognize that we serve a God of times and seasons who appoints ‘ weeks of harvest for his people ’ (Eccl.3:1-11;Jer.5:24)
Also, we are saved today because He has ordained us unto eternal life (Acts13:48) and we are redeemed because He has predestined us unto salvation (Rm.8:29-30)

God also led Isaac to where his enviable blessings were and inspite of all oppositions,he realized them (Gen.26:1-14)

But remember, only the spiritual can be spiritually led (Rev.1:10;ICor.2:14) and only the meek can be guided (Pro.25:9;Matt.5:5).
I believe as we remain spiritually sensitive to His leadings, He will guide us with His hands to where our supernatural blessings are waiting (Ps.32:8;Jer:48:17)
Wisdom

Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card's content.

Wisdom

The Bible says in Dan. 12:3:
"And they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars forever and ever."
Wisdom is said to be the principal thing in the book of Proverbs (Prov. 4:7-11). It goes on to say, when we embrace wisdom, it shall promote (change our position for the better). It goes further to say, a crown of glory shall she deliver unto us and an ornament of grace.
From the above, it is clear that glory is one of the products of wisdom. Paul the Apostle, in his epistle to the Corinthians, said:

"Howbeit we speak wisdom among them that are perfect: yet not the wisdom of this world, nor of the princes of this world, that come to nought: But we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery, even the hidden wisdom, which God ordained before the world unto our glory" (1 Cor. 2:6-7).

However, there are four kinds of wisdom as outlined in the epistle of James:
"This wisdom descendeth not from above, but is earthly, sensual, devilish. For where envying and strife is, there is confusion and every evil work. But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and easy to be intreated, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality, and without hypocrisy" (James 3:15-17).
From the above scripture, we can classify wisdom into four categories:

1-Earthly – natural wisdom, what we call common sense. You don’t have to learn how to eat, drink or sleep; they are all common-sense issues.
2-Then we have intellectual wisdom – sensual. This is what we go to school to acquire in various disciplines – engineering, technology, social sciences, humanities, law, etc.
3-Then we have devilish wisdom which talks about occultic, diabolical wisdom.
4-And finally, we have the wisdom from above, which is the wisdom of God, and remember the Bible says whatever is from above is above all (John 3:31). The wisdom of God is superior to all other kinds of wisdom because it is from above.

Our access to this wisdom is clearly outlined in scriptures:

1-It is for the redeemed (Matt. 11:19). The child of a goat possesses the wisdom of a goat. The child of a duck possesses the wisdom of the duck. The child of a cow possesses the wisdom of a cow, and so on and so forth. This is why only the children of God can access the wisdom of God.
2-We access this wisdom by prayer, "If anyone lack wisdom, let him ask of God..." (James 1:5). Also, we saw Daniel take delivery of divine wisdom through prayer (Dan. 2:16-19).
3-We also access this wisdom through learning. "A wise man shall hear and increase learning" (Prov. 1:5). Wisdom has been defined as the correct application of knowledge. That means knowledge is the raw material for the production of wisdom. Concerning Timothy, Paul wrote:
"And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures, which are able to make thee wise unto salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus" (2 Tim. 3:15).
4-Furthermore, we access divine wisdom through meditation (Ps. 119:97-100):
"O how love I thy law! it is my meditation all the day. Thou through thy commandments hast made me wiser than mine enemies: for they are ever with me. I have more understanding than all my teachers: for thy testimonies are my meditation. I understand more than the ancients, because I keep thy precepts. "
And through the process of meditation David said, "You have made me wiser than my enemies. I have more understanding than all my teachers." Meditation is key to operating in divine wisdom.
5-And finally, through keeping wise company. "He that walks with the wise shall be wise, but a companion of fools shall be destroyed" (Prov. 13:20). How? Iron sharpens iron, so a man sharpens the countenance (wisdom) of his friends" (Prov. 27:17).

Proofs of divine wisdom at work:
1-It is Creative:
"O LORD, how manifold are thy works! In wisdom hast thou made them all: the earth is full of thy riches" (Ps. 104:24; Prov. 3:19).
2-It commands exploits (Matt. 13:54; Mk. 6:2).
3-It stabilizes (Isa. 33:6; Mk. 7:27-28).
Operating by divine wisdom is building on the rock; the wind and storms and the rain notwithstanding, the building shall remain.

(also Mk. 16:17-18; Isa. 8:18)

But according to the Holy Scriptures, we do not wait for miracles, we work them!
"And thou shalt take this rod in thine hand, wherewith thou shalt do signs" (Exo. 4:17).
(also 1 Cor. 12:10)

"Long time therefore abode they speaking boldly in the Lord, which gave testimony unto the word of his grace, and granted signs and wonders to be done by their hands" (Acts 14:3).
(also Ps. 107:20; Matt. 8:8; Lk. 5:1-5, 17)

"Go; wash in the pool of Siloam..." He went his way therefore, and washed, and came seeing. (John 9:7)

Again, Jesus, after His resurrection, stood on the shore, although His disciples knew not that it was Jesus, and He said unto them,
"Children, have ye any meat?" They answered him, No. And he said unto them,
"Cast the net on the right side of the ship, and ye shall find." They cast therefore, and now they were not able to draw it for the multitude of fishes." (John 21:5-6)
Therefore, from the gospel of John as enumerated above, we discover that obedience of faith is the master key to the world of the miraculous.
We therefore believe that because Israel came out of Egypt on the wings of signs and wonders, that as you align in obedience of faith to the revelation of the word, you are also coming out of every kind of Egypt. (Ps. 105:26-44; Exo. 3:21; Exo. 12:36)
Also, because Israel came into the Promised Land – a land flowing with milk and honey – on the wings of signs and wonders, you are coming into your prophetic promised land. (Deut. 26:8-9)
New birth is a birth into the supernatural world. When one is born again, he/she is born into a world of signs and wonders. Every child of God is a miracle in the world and to the world (Zech. 3:8).

It is written,
"He that believeth and is baptized shall be saved... and these signs shall follow them that believe..." (Mark 16:16-17).

It is also written,
"the wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh and whither it goeth, so is everyone that is born of the Spirit" (John 3:8).

When the first century apostles took the stage, the people exclaimed,
"the gods are come down to us in the likeness of men" (Acts 14:11).

This is why the Word says prophetically concerning the redeemed,
"Behold, I and the children whom the Lord hath given me, are for signs and for wonders" (Isaiah 8:18).

Anytime one is challenged and seems to have come to his wits’ end, people will always assert, ‘it will take a miracle’.
What it will take for our trials to be turned into testimonies and our obstacles to miracles is right in our hands – a miracle.

But every miracle is a confirmation of the Word. The Bible says,
"...the Lord also working with them and confirming the word with signs following" (Mark 16:20).

It is also written that,
"...God gave testimony unto the Word of His grace and granted signs and wonders to be done by their hands" (Acts 14:3).
The Holy Spirit

According to scriptures, every child of God is born into a world of no-limits...

The Holy Spirit

According to scriptures, every child of God:

1.Is born into a world of no-limits – Jn 14:12, 1 Jn 4:17, Mk 9:23, Mk 10:27, Deut 28:1-13, Matt 5:17
2.Is born for the topmost top – Rev 22:16 (Jn 17:18), Dan 12:3 (Matt 11:19, 1 Cor 2:16), 1 Kg 4:29-34 (Matt 12:42, Jn 17:18)
3.Has an heritage of unending progress in life – Prv 4:18 (1 Cor 5:21), 2 Cor 3:18 (2 Cor 5:21)
4.His breakthrough as a seed of Abraham is as far as his eyes can see – Gen 13:14-15, Is 51:1-3, Ps 119:18, Rev 3:17-18 (Eph 1:17-18/Eph 3:8-11)

From all the above, every child of God is ordained to live in a world of no-limits.
But as it was with Christ, even though He was conscious of His mission from the age of twelve, yet He could not deliver His mandate until He was empowered by the Spirit – Lk 4:14-27/37.
Therefore, our golden destiny in this golden age of the church cannot be fully realized without the empowerment of the Spirit.
The disciples also were under the teaching and mentorship of Christ for three and a half years, yet Jesus commanded them not to embark on their mission until they were endued with power from on high (Lk 24:49, Act 1:8).
And after the empowerment of the Holy Ghost, they began to command unstoppable breakthroughs – Acts 2:1-41/42-47, Acts 4:4, Acts 5:14, Acts 6:7.

From all the above, it is evident that supernatural breakthrough is impossible for any believer without the ministry of the Holy Ghost at work. But why? You may ask. This is because all the forces of supernatural breakthroughs are embedded in the Holy Ghost – 1 Cor 16:9, Lk 10:19, Ps 89:20-24, Ps 105:13-15, Eze 37:1-14, Num 11:31-33.

We all know that the only language the devil understands is the language of power. As it is written,
"Thou through the greatness of thy power shall thine enemies submit themselves unto thee" (Ps. 66:3).

We also understand from scriptures that power is in degrees and anointing is in levels (Ezek. 47:1-10, Ps. 45:6-8).

Why do we need power? We need power for the following reasons among others:

1.To fulfill our mission on the earth (Lk. 4:14-18, Acts 1:8) 2.For protection (Ps. 105:13-15, 1 Pet. 1:5) 3.For victory over the battles of life (Lk. 10:19, Ps. 66:3) 4.To command dominion (Ps. 110:1-3, Is. 45:1-3) 5.To preserve the blessings of God on our lives (Ps. 112:1-9, 92:10-15)
But power is always released at a cost. However, it does not cost money, but it costs something much more demanding.
And when Simon saw that through laying on of the apostles' hands the Holy Ghost was given, he offered them money, But Peter said unto him,
"Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money" (Acts 8:18-20).

Again in the parable of the ten virgins, when the five foolish virgins asked if they could be spared some oil,
"But the wise answered, saying, Not so; lest there be not enough for us and you: but go ye rather to them that sell, and buy for yourselves" (Matt 25:9).

Do you want to see more of God's power in your life? Do you want to grow in power? Then it will cost you the following:

-More consecration – (Ps. 45:6-8, Lk. 5:37-38, Pr. 1:23)
-A genuine thirst – (Ps. 63:1-3, Is. 44:3-4, John 7:37-38)
-More of fasting and prayer – (Lk. 4:1-14, 9:28-43, Matt. 17:20-21)
-More attention to the word – (Lev. 6:9-13, Acts 6:1-3) etc.
Healing

We understand from scriptures that until sin came, sickness was nowhere to be found...

Healing

We understand from scriptures that until sin came, sickness was nowhere to be found. The first Adam knew no sickness before the fall. How do I know? You may ask.
There was no provision made for his healthcare because sickness was not part of his nature. All the herbs were for his food, not for drugs. Therefore, when sin is gone, sickness becomes illegal. (Gen 1:29)
The Bible says,
"I wish above all things that thou mayest prosper and be in health even as thy soul prospereth" (3 John 2).
When your soul is healthy, your body must be healthy also.

The price for our total health has been fully paid. It is therefore your covenant right to enjoy divine health. The Bible says,
"You have been bought with a price; therefore glorify God in your body and in your spirit, which are God’s" (1 Cor. 6:20).
And just like sin does not glorify God in the soul, sickness does not glorify God in your body.

The body of Christ is increasingly challenged by the spirit of infirmities in all forms. But,
"Is there no balm in Gilead? Is there no physician there? Why then is not the health of the daughter of my people recovered?" (Jer. 8:22).
Christ is not only the savior of our soul; He is also the savior of our body (Eph. 5:30).

"And by His stripes, we were healed" (1 Peter 2:24).
God said in His word,
"I am the Lord that healeth thee" (Exo. 15:26).

Furthermore, we must recognize that God is committed to our total health; sicknesses and diseases are not our portion in His Kingdom.
We must equally recognize that plagues and afflictions are not meant for us, and all forms of oppression of the wicked are illegal in our lives.
God is saying that,
"It is our right in redemption to prosper and be in health" (3 John 2).

We should acknowledge that Christ Himself took our sicknesses and bore our infirmities (Matt. 8:17).
And that by His stripes, we were healed (1 Pet. 2:24).
Therefore, total health is our covenant right (Exo. 23:25-26; John 15:1-5).
Long life is our heritage (Isa. 65:20-24; Gen. 25:8; Gal. 3:13-14; Gal. 4:28; Ps. 91:16).
That death has no more legal power over our lives (Heb. 2:14-15, 1 Cor. 15:26; 1 Cor. 15:55-57; Isa. 25:6-9; Hos 13:14; Exo. 12:12-13).

Christ is our substitute in death as He was to Barabbas (Luke 23:23-25).
We cannot have God as our healer and still remain bound in sickness; for with God all things are possible.

We must continue to expect the great physician to administer His balm on us and our household through the following channels among others:
1-By the Word (Ps. 107:20).
2-By the Anointing (Rom. 8:11, James 5:13-14).
3-By the Communion (1 Cor. 11:23-30, John 6:48-58).
4-By Prophetic Impartations (Acts 19:11-12, Rev. 12:1).
Praise

Every time thanksgiving goes up to heaven, we secure God’s attention in prayers...

Praise

Every time thanksgiving goes up to heaven, we secure God’s attention in prayers; because when we ask anything according to His will, He hears us. (Phil. 4:6, 1 John 5:14)
Therefore, remember:
-No one ever gets stranded with thanksgiving (John 6:6-11)
-Praise will always provoke divine favour (Mark 6:21-23)
-Nothing is ever late with thanksgiving (John 11:40-44)
-It may appear stinking now; thanksgiving will turn it to sweet smelling savour (1 Thess. 5:18, Heb. 10:38) Praise will always culminate in supernatural harvest (Ps. 67:5-7)

Also remember, God still crowns the year with His goodness and all His paths drop fatness.
We must also be reminded that taking offenses in God is a risk, but thanksgiving and praise is a covenant cure for offenses (Luke 17:1-2, Matt. 11:6). Hence, to take full delivery of our breakthrough agenda, the Word of God admonishes:
* Although the fig tree shall not blossom, neither shall fruit be in the vines;
* the labour of the olives shall fail, and the fields shall yield no meat;
* the flock shall be cut off from the fold, and there shall be no herd in the stalls:
* Yet I will rejoice in the LORD, I will joy in the God of my salvation.
* The LORD God is my strength, and He will make my feet like hinds' feet, and He will make me to walk upon mine high places. To the chief singer on my stringed instruments. —Hab. 3:17-19
In order for our harvest not to perish, we must maintain the joy of harvest through intensive thanksgiving and praise (Joel 1:11-12, Isa. 9:3).
Praise is a missing axe-head in the body of Christ today. The lack of the spiritual understanding of this weapon has incapacitated many Christians. Just imagine, how do you fell a tree with an axe that has no axe-head? (2 Kings 6:5)
We have misconstrued praise as a weapon of acknowledging God’s doings in our life—that is thanksgiving, that is not praise. Praise is a spiritual means of provoking divine intervention. It is not what we do after things have happened; it is what we do to make things happen.
And when they began to sing and to praise, the LORD set ambushments against the children of Ammon, Moab, and mount Seir, which were come against Judah; and they were smitten (2 Chron. 20:22).
This was the same experience that Paul and Silas had in the prison (Acts 16:25-30).
King David—a praising giant—was also a gallant overcomer—he never lost any battle. David said, "I will bless the LORD at all times: His praises shall continually be in my mouth" (Ps. 34:1, Ps. 119:164).

The following things happen when we praise God acceptably:
-We invoke divine presence (Ps. 22:3)
-We provoke the release of the anointing for conquest (Ps. 89:20-24, Ps. 92:10-15)
A combination of the presence and the power of God will bring down any mountain in our life. Praise is therefore a most vital force in the realm of the Spirit.
The same God who took over the battle of Jehoshaphat will take over the battle of individuals this month, and all our enemies shall be smitten, and not one of them shall escape (2 Chron. 20:17-24). But how can God take over what we did not hand over?
In the same vein, God who stepped into the prison of Paul and Silas will step into our prison and bring everyone out in colours (Acts 16:25-30). But how can God step into our prison when we are not praising Him?
